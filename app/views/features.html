Example use-cases
These are only a few of the possible applications

Open innovation

Why

Innovation challenges do not utilize the most promising aspect of crowdsourcing: synergy through collaboration.

What

By adding more collaborative elements to the competition process, the crowd can build upon eachothers contribution. Leading to results that no single contributor could have produced by him/herself.

How

Have the contributors vote on, comment on and play with eachothers work. And divide the prize money more fairly among the (top) contributors using the Mobbr button.

From microtasking to macrotasking

Why

Microtasking platforms lack the payment functionality to be able to facilitate more complex tasks.

What

Through using Mobbr's crowd payment functionality, those platforms can vastly expand their market reach by facilitating more complicated 'macro-tasks' in collaborative processes.

How

Don't prepackage the work at a fixed price, but put it in an open platform and steer on the desired output. Design the process to let the users rate the quality of the work and pay everyone their fair share using Mobbr.

Knowledge sharing

Why

For most companies, fully utilizing their collective knowledge remains a difficult challenge.

What

Adding payment functionality to knowledge sharing tools, allows for the necessary incentives to share and monetize knowledge, instead of protecting it.

How

Post knowledge work or tasks online using simple Wiki- or Q&A software. Pay employees, consultants and/or external experts their fair share for contributing using the Mobbr button.

The crowdsourced helpdesk

Why

Helpdesks face the continuous challenge of improving satisfaction targets whilst lowering costs.

What

By simply integrating the Mobbr button those helpdesks can enable crowdsourcing of customer service in a way that is more personal and much more efficient. After all, the crowd never takes a break.

How

Open up the helpdesk to the crowd and use the Mobbr button to pledge money to specific questions, categories or tags. In addition, people can reward eachother.

Blogging platforms

Why

User generated content sites are often too dependent on (diminishing) advertising income..

What

By adding Mobbr buttons to blogs, articles and other content, the platform can generate alternative sources of income.

How

Crowdsource a content marketing campaign and distribute part of the revenue, ask readers for donations, or create content on demand using the Mobbr button for (micro)crowdfunding. Just to name a few examples..

Expert communities

When

Online communities of experts generally do not fully leverage their collective wisdom and workforce.

What

Those communities can create income streams by bringing together supply (i.e. the expert community) and demand (i.e. businesses).

How

Facilitate the execution of work, tasks and projects relating to your community's expertise. Simply add a crowdsourcing or collaboration tool and Mobbr button to the website to facilitate the process.
